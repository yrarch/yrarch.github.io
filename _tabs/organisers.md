  **Sinem Hacıosmanoğlu** (Universität Tübingen)  
  Sinem is a PhD candidate at the University of Tübingen, Germany, Department of Geoscience, Institute for Archeological Sciences. She completed her bachelor (BA) and master (MSc) in Istanbul Technical University, Turkey, at the Department of Geological Engineering. Currently, she is engaged with her PhD thesis an investigation of clay resources and ancient ceramics from Late Bronze Age and Iron Age in Cilicia, Southeast Anatolia, Eastern Mediterranean by using several disciplines of mineralogy, petrography, geochemistry and field geology by the grant from Swiss National Science Foundation (SNF-Project). In addition, she is working as a research assistant on several projects at German Research Foundation (DFG) and the Competence Center Archaeometry - Baden-Württemberg (CCA-BW).

  **Baptiste Solard** (Universität Tübingen)  
  Baptiste completed in November 2019 his Diplôme d’ingénieur chimiste (BSc and MSc equivalent) in Material Sciences at the National Chemistry School of Montpellier, France (ENSCM). In October 2021 he successfully finished his master (MSc) of Archaeological Sciences with Archaeometry specialisation on Atticising Black Gloss from the 4th century BCE at the University of Tübingen, Germany, Department of Geoscience, Institute for Archeological Sciences. Since December 2019, he has been working as a research assistant at the Competence Center Archaeometry – Baden-Württemberg (CCA-BW) on various projects on ancient ceramic technology. In the course of 2023, he will start his PhD at the University of Tübingen on pigmented glass productions from the Merovingian period in Switzerland.
